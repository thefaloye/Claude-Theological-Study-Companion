# Theologians Canon — Theological Study Companion

Extended reference list of approved theologians and scholars. Load this file when needing detailed guidance on which theologian to cite, their primary works, and their specific areas of contribution.

**Core rule**: Only reference a theologian where they are **directly and accurately relevant** to the subject. Accuracy of relevance takes precedence over breadth of citation.

---

## Patristic Era (c. 100–600 AD)

### Augustine of Hippo (354–430)
**Tradition**: North African Bishop; proto-Reformed on grace and predestination
**Primary Works**: *Confessions*, *The City of God*, *On the Trinity*, *On the Spirit and the Letter*, Anti-Pelagian writings
**Relevance**: Soteriology (grace, predestination, free will), Trinity, ecclesiology, hermeneutics (De Doctrina Christiana), original sin. The most-cited Father in Reformed theology.

### John Chrysostom (347–407)
**Tradition**: Antiochene school; Bishop of Constantinople
**Primary Works**: Homilies on Matthew, John, Romans, Corinthians, Galatians; *On the Priesthood*
**Relevance**: NT exegesis (especially Pauline). Antiochene literal-grammatical method anticipates Reformed hermeneutics. Cite for exegetical tradition, not dogmatic precision.

### Athanasius of Alexandria (c. 296–373)
**Primary Works**: *On the Incarnation*, *Against the Arians*, *Life of Antony*
**Relevance**: Christology and the doctrine of the Trinity (Nicene controversy). Essential for any engagement with the homoousion and Arian dispute.

### Irenaeus of Lyons (c. 130–202)
**Primary Works**: *Against Heresies*, *Demonstration of the Apostolic Preaching*
**Relevance**: Biblical theology — recapitulation theory, Adam-Christ typology, canon of Scripture. One of the earliest redemptive-historical thinkers.

### Basil of Caesarea (329–379)
**Primary Works**: *On the Holy Spirit*, Letters, *Hexaemeron*
**Relevance**: Pneumatology (doctrine of the Holy Spirit), Trinitarian theology, creation.

### Gregory of Nazianzus (329–390)
**Primary Works**: *Five Theological Orations*, Letters
**Relevance**: Trinitarian theology, Christology (especially the two natures). His formula *"what is not assumed is not healed"* is critical for the doctrine of the incarnation.

### Jerome (347–420)
**Primary Works**: *Vulgate* translation, Commentaries, Letters
**Relevance**: Biblical languages and textual criticism. Important for the history of Bible translation and OT canon.

### Cyprian of Carthage (c. 200–258)
**Relevance**: Ecclesiology, unity of the church, baptism debates in early church.

### Tertullian (c. 155–220)
**Caution**: Use only from his **pre-Montanist period**. His Montanist writings on prophecy and the Paraclete should not be cited approvingly.
**Relevance**: Early Latin theological vocabulary (*Trinitas*, *substantia*), apologetics, canon.

### Clement of Alexandria (c. 150–215)
**Relevance**: Early Christian hermeneutics, apologetics to Greco-Roman culture. Use with care — his allegorism is extensive.

### Origen of Alexandria (c. 185–253)
**Caution**: Significant theological errors (subordinationism, apokatastasis). **Use for exegetical insights only**, and flag his theological positions critically. Do not cite him approvingly on soteriology, eschatology, or the nature of Scripture.
**Relevance**: Early hermeneutics, textual criticism (Hexapla), extensive commentary tradition.

## Medieval Era (c. 600–1500)

### Thomas Aquinas (1225–1274)
**Tradition**: Dominican; Scholastic
**Primary Works**: *Summa Theologiae*, *Summa Contra Gentiles*, *Commentary on the Sentences*
**Engagement**: Critical but fair. Aquinas provides important categories for theological precision (essence/existence, substance/accidents, act/potency). Reformed theologians engage him extensively (Turretin, Bavinck, Frame). Note points of divergence: merit, infused grace, analogy of being.

### Anselm of Canterbury (1033–1109)
**Primary Works**: *Cur Deus Homo* (Why God Became Man), *Proslogion*, *Monologion*
**Relevance**: Satisfaction theory of atonement (*Cur Deus Homo* — foundational for penal substitution debates), ontological argument for God's existence.

### Bernard of Clairvaux (1090–1153)
**Primary Works**: *On Loving God*, Sermons on the Song of Songs
**Relevance**: Christological devotion, mystical theology (use carefully — note his Marian excess). Luther valued him; Calvin quoted him.

## Reformation Era (c. 1500–1600)

### John Calvin (1509–1564)
**Tradition**: Reformed; Geneva
**Primary Works**: *Institutes of the Christian Religion* (1559 final ed.), *Commentaries* (on most of Scripture), *Tracts and Letters*
**Status**: **Primary Reformed voice**. Cite throughout. His exegetical method (brevity and lucidity) shaped the tradition. The Institutes remain the benchmark of Reformed systematic theology.

### Martin Luther (1483–1546)
**Primary Works**: *Luther's Works* (55 vols.), *Small Catechism*, *Large Catechism*, *The Bondage of the Will*, *Commentary on Galatians*
**Note**: Lutheran, not Reformed. **Note divergences**: consubstantiation (not transubstantiation, but not Reformed memorialism either), Lutheran two-kingdoms, law/gospel dialectic differs from Reformed use of law. Strong on justification, Scripture, grace.

### Heinrich Bullinger (1504–1575)
**Primary Works**: *Decades*, *Second Helvetic Confession*, *De Testamento*
**Relevance**: Covenant theology development, Reformed confessional tradition, Zurich Reformation.

### Theodore Beza (1519–1605)
**Relevance**: High Calvinist soteriology, supralapsarianism, successor to Calvin in Geneva.

---

## Post-Reformation Orthodox Era (c. 1600–1750)

### Francis Turretin (1623–1687)
**Primary Works**: *Institutes of Elenctic Theology* (3 vols.)
**Status**: **Primary Post-Reformation dogmatic theologian**. The *Institutes of Elenctic Theology* is the standard of Reformed scholastic orthodoxy. Cite as a primary authority on any systematic theological question. Used as textbook at Princeton Seminary for generations.

### Herman Witsius (1636–1708)
**Primary Works**: *The Economy of the Covenants Between God and Man* (2 vols.)
**Relevance**: Covenant theology, covenant of works, covenant of grace. Essential for Reformed federal theology.

### Wilhelmus à Brakel (1635–1711)
**Primary Works**: *The Christian's Reasonable Service* (4 vols.)
**Relevance**: Pastoral dogmatics, experiential Reformed theology, practical piety. Dutch Second Reformation (*Nadere Reformatie*).

### Zacharias Ursinus (1534–1583)
**Primary Works**: *Commentary on the Heidelberg Catechism*
**Relevance**: Heidelberg Catechism, covenant theology, Reformed catechetics.

### John Owen (1616–1683)
**Tradition**: Particular Baptist / Congregationalist; England
**Primary Works**: *Works* (24 vols.), *The Death of Death in the Death of Christ*, *Of Communion with God*, *Pneumatologia* (Holy Spirit), *Hebrews Commentary* (7 vols.), *The Glory of Christ*
**Status**: **Primary authority** for pneumatology, Christology, covenant, definite atonement, and the inner life of faith. Particularly important for the 1689 confessional tradition.

### John Bunyan (1628–1688)
**Primary Works**: *Pilgrim's Progress*, *Holy War*, *Grace Abounding to the Chief of Sinners*
**Relevance**: Practical theology, perseverance, assurance. Confessionally aligned with 1689.

### Thomas Goodwin (1600–1680)
**Primary Works**: *Works* (12 vols.), *Christ Set Forth*, *The Heart of Christ*
**Relevance**: Christology, assurance, Ephesians. One of the Westminster divines.

### Thomas Watson (c. 1620–1686)
**Primary Works**: *A Body of Divinity*, *The Ten Commandments*, *The Lord's Prayer*, *All Things for Good*
**Relevance**: Experiential Reformed preaching, Westminster Shorter Catechism exposition.

### Stephen Charnock (1628–1680)
**Primary Works**: *Discourses upon the Existence and Attributes of God*
**Relevance**: Theology proper — the divine attributes. Standard reference for Reformed doctrine of God.

---

## 18th–19th Century

### Jonathan Edwards (1703–1758)
**Primary Works**: *Freedom of the Will*, *Original Sin*, *Religious Affections*, *The End for Which God Created the World*, *A History of the Work of Redemption*
**Relevance**: Soteriology, revival theology, theology of history, religious experience evaluated biblically. *A History of the Work of Redemption* is an early biblical theology.

### Charles Hodge (1797–1878)
**Primary Works**: *Systematic Theology* (3 vols.), *Commentaries on Romans, Ephesians, Corinthians*
**Relevance**: Old Princeton Reformed systematics. Comprehensive and confessionally faithful.

### B.B. Warfield (1851–1921)
**Primary Works**: *The Inspiration and Authority of the Bible*, *The Plan of Salvation*, *Counterfeit Miracles*, *Calvin and Calvinism*
**Relevance**: Bibliology (inspiration, inerrancy), soteriology, critique of charismatic claims. *Counterfeit Miracles* is the locus classicus for cessationist argument from church history.

### J.C. Ryle (1816–1900)
**Primary Works**: *Holiness*, *Expository Thoughts on the Gospels*, *Knots Untied*
**Relevance**: Practical holiness, Gospel exposition, Anglican evangelicalism.

### Abraham Kuyper (1837–1920)
**Primary Works**: *Lectures on Calvinism*, *The Work of the Holy Spirit*, *Principles of Sacred Theology*
**Relevance**: Reformed worldview, common grace, pneumatology, cultural engagement.

---

## Modern Reformed Era (20th–21st Century)

### Herman Bavinck (1854–1921)
**Primary Works**: *Reformed Dogmatics* (4 vols.), *The Doctrine of God*, *Our Reasonable Faith*
**Status**: **Primary modern systematic theology**. The most comprehensive Reformed dogmatics of the modern era. Cite as the standard modern Reformed systematic reference.

### Louis Berkhof (1873–1957)
**Primary Works**: *Systematic Theology*, *Principles of Biblical Interpretation*
**Relevance**: Standard seminary-level Reformed systematics. Clear, concise, confessionally faithful.

### John Murray (1898–1975)
**Primary Works**: *Redemption Accomplished and Applied*, *The Epistle to the Romans* (commentary), *Principles of Conduct*, *Collected Writings* (4 vols.)
**Relevance**: Soteriology, biblical ethics, covenant, NT theology of justification and sanctification.

### D. Martyn Lloyd-Jones (1899–1981)
**Primary Works**: *Sermons on Romans* (14 vols.), *Studies in the Sermon on the Mount*, *Spiritual Depression*, *Preaching and Preachers*
**Relevance**: Expository preaching, Reformed homiletics, pastoral theology, revival.

### R.C. Sproul (1939–2017)
**Primary Works**: *The Holiness of God*, *Chosen by God*, *Faith Alone*, *Everyone's a Theologian*
**Relevance**: Reformed apologetics, soteriology, theology proper, accessible Calvinism.

### Joel Beeke (1952–)
**Primary Works**: *Reformed Preaching*, *A Puritan Theology* (with Mark Jones), *Living for God's Glory*
**Relevance**: Puritan and experiential Reformed theology, preaching, piety.

### John Frame (1939–)
**Primary Works**: *The Doctrine of God*, *The Doctrine of the Word of God*, *Systematic Theology*, *A History of Western Philosophy and Theology*
**Relevance**: Theology proper, epistemology, perspectivalism, Van Tillian apologetics.

### Robert Letham (1947–)
**Primary Works**: *The Holy Trinity*, *The Work of Christ*, *Systematic Theology*, *Union with Christ*
**Relevance**: Trinity, Christology, union with Christ, Reformed systematics.

### Geerhardus Vos (1862–1949)
**Primary Works**: *Biblical Theology: Old and New Testaments*, *The Pauline Eschatology*, *Redemptive History and Biblical Interpretation*
**Relevance**: **Father of Reformed biblical theology**. Essential for any redemptive-historical work. His organic conception of revelation development is foundational to the integrated hermeneutic of this skill.

### Graeme Goldsworthy (1934–)
**Primary Works**: *According to Plan*, *Gospel and Kingdom*, *Preaching the Whole Bible as Christian Scripture*
**Relevance**: Biblical theology, Christ-centred hermeneutics, preaching from the OT.

### Edmund Clowney (1917–2005)
**Primary Works**: *Preaching Christ in All of Scripture*, *The Church*, *The Unfolding Mystery*
**Relevance**: Typology, Christ-centred preaching, biblical theology of the church.

### G.K. Beale (1949–)
**Primary Works**: *The Temple and the Church's Mission*, *A New Testament Biblical Theology*, *Handbook on the New Testament Use of the Old Testament* (with D.A. Carson)
**Relevance**: OT in NT, typology, Revelation commentary, biblical theology.

### D.A. Carson (1946–)
**Primary Works**: *Exegetical Fallacies*, *The Gospel According to John* (Pillar), *Showing the Spirit*, *The God Who Is There*
**Relevance**: NT exegesis, hermeneutics, Johannine theology, Spirit and gifts (balanced cessationist).

### Thomas Schreiner (1954–)
**Primary Works**: *Paul, Apostle of God's Glory in Christ*, *New Testament Theology*, Commentaries on Romans, Galatians, Hebrews, Peter
**Relevance**: Pauline theology, soteriology, NT theology, complementarianism.
